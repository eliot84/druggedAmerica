Foodborne Outbreaks 1998 - 2008 by State

Manure is valuable to the farming industry, but at large production quantities they become problematic. The Environmental Protection Agency has classified the single most pressing public health issue associated with CAFO locations specifically being the amount of manure they produce. Although manure contain plant nutrients such as nitrogen and phosphorus, they also contain a variety of potential contaminants including bacterial and viral pathogens. Although sewage treatment plants are required for human waste, no such treatment facility exists for livestock waste at these CAFO locations.
